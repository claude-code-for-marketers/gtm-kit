# ICP Definition — Newsletter Radar

*This is the canonical ICP document. All campaigns, scoring models, and outreach sequences reference this file. Update it when your understanding of the ideal customer evolves — and note the date of each change.*

Last updated: 2026-06-16 · Status: **pre-launch** · v0 ICP ready for first account scoring; validate against design-partner conversations.

> **Who we sell to:** brands and advertisers that buy (or should be buying) newsletter sponsorships —
> the *buy side*. **Not** newsletter publishers selling ad slots (that's the incumbents' market and our anti-ICP).

---

## Ideal Customer Profile

The buyer is a company that already spends, or is about to spend, on newsletter advertising and needs
to plan it like a real channel — where to spend, what competitors are doing, and what's working.

### Firmographics

| Dimension | Ideal Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Company type | B2B SaaS, AI / dev tools, fintech / finance, business & productivity (+ crypto) | Must advertise in the verticals we crawl |
| Employee count | 20–500 | Big enough to have a marketing budget, small enough to feel media-planning pain |
| Funding stage | Seed → Series C | Funded growth companies with paid-acquisition budgets |
| Funding recency | Raised in last 12 months | Fresh budget → new channel experimentation |
| Marketing maturity | Has dedicated growth/performance marketing | They run paid channels and benchmark spend |
| Geography | US first | Largest newsletter ad market and easiest first outbound motion |

> **Coverage constraint:** our crawl covers **tech, AI, business, finance** newsletters (+ adjacent like
> crypto). An advertiser is only a fit if its buyers read newsletters in those verticals. A consumer/DTC
> brand whose audience lives in lifestyle/parenting newsletters is *out of ICP until we cover that vertical.*

### Behavioral / technographic fit

**Strong fit indicators:**
- Already running newsletter sponsorships (detectable in our own index) — the strongest signal of all
- Heavy on other paid channels (Meta/Google/podcast ads) and looking to diversify
- A competitor is visibly active in newsletters (they'll want to benchmark)
- Uses media-planning / attribution tooling (signals they measure channels seriously)

**Weak fit indicators:**
- No paid-acquisition motion at all (not ready to plan a channel)
- Pure brand/awareness spender with no measurement culture
- Already locked into a managed agency that owns all planning (deprioritize direct sale; could become a later channel-partner path)

### Organizational signals

- Has a Head of Growth / Performance Marketing / Demand Gen
- Recently hired a paid-media or growth marketer (new channel mandate)
- Marketing team large enough that media planning is someone's explicit job

## Tier Definitions

### Tier 1 — Dream Accounts

Advertisers already spending meaningfully on newsletters who feel the planning pain *today*.

**Criteria:**
- Detected as an active newsletter sponsor across 2+ newsletters in our index
- Has a dedicated growth/performance marketing function
- A direct competitor is also visibly advertising (benchmarking demand)

**Outreach approach:** Manual, insight-led — lead with *their own* footprint and a competitor comparison.

---

### Tier 2 — High-Fit Accounts

Strong paid-acquisition advertisers not yet in newsletters, or just starting.

**Criteria:**
- Heavy spend on Meta/Google/podcast ads
- Raised funding in the last 12 months
- In a vertical we cover (tech, AI, business, finance, crypto)

**Outreach approach:** Signal-triggered, semi-personalized — lead with where similar/competitor brands advertise.

---

### Tier 3 — Good-Fit Accounts

Meets minimum criteria; activate with automated, lightly personalized outreach.

**Criteria:**
- 20+ employees with a marketing function
- In a vertical we cover (tech, AI, business, finance)
- Some evidence of paid acquisition

**Outreach approach:** Automated sequences, template-based, segmented by vertical.

---

### Tier 4 — Monitor Only

Too early, no paid motion, or not yet detectable in any ad channel. Stay on radar; don't activate.

## Anti-ICP

| Exclusion | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| **Newsletter publishers / creators** | They're the incumbents' buyers; we are **buy-side only** |
| Brands outside our crawled verticals | Audience not in tech/AI/business/finance newsletters → no useful data for them yet |
| Companies with no paid-acquisition motion | Nothing to plan; not ready for channel intelligence |
| Sub-20-employee pre-revenue startups | No budget, no dedicated marketer |
| Pure-brand spenders with no measurement culture | Won't value benchmarking/recommendations |
| Ad-sales agencies (sell-side) | Wrong side of the market (buy-side media agencies *are* in ICP) |

---

## Qualification Framework

### Must-have (deal-breaker if absent)
1. Runs paid acquisition (newsletters or other channels)
2. Has, or is hiring for, a growth/performance/demand-gen owner
3. Operates in a vertical we crawl (tech, AI, business, finance — where newsletter advertising exists at scale)

### Strong indicators (2+ = high confidence)
1. Already detected as a newsletter sponsor in our index
2. A direct competitor is actively advertising in newsletters
3. Raised funding in the last 12 months
4. Diversifying away from saturated paid social/search

### Red flags (2+ = deprioritize)
1. No measurement/attribution culture
2. Newsletter advertising irrelevant to their audience (e.g., hyper-local services)
3. All media buying outsourced to an agency that won't change tools
