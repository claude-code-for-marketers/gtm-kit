# Persona: Founder / Solo Marketing Lead (small team)

*The economic buyer at early/small ICP accounts that don't yet have a dedicated growth hire. Status: pre-launch — validate with design partners.*

---

## Overview

**Title(s):** Founder/CEO, Co-founder, Head of Marketing (team of 1–3), Marketing Lead
**Typical seniority:** Founder / first marketing hire
**Decision role:** Economic buyer + champion (they wear both hats)
**Found at companies:** Seed–Series A DTC/SaaS/consumer brands, 20–100 employees, lean marketing team

---

## What They Care About

**Their primary metric:** Growth efficiency with limited time and headcount — every channel test has to pay off fast.

**Their biggest problem right now:** They suspect newsletters could work but can't afford to learn by trial
and error. They want to copy what's already working for similar/competitor brands without hiring an expert.

**What a good week looks like:** A cheap, fast channel experiment that worked — found via someone else's proven playbook.

**What a bad week looks like:** Wasted a week and a chunk of budget testing a placement that was never going to fit their audience.

---

## How They Buy

**Involvement in purchase:** Initiates, approves, and uses it themselves
**Discovery behavior:** Founder communities, indie-hacker/DTC newsletters, word of mouth, Twitter/LinkedIn
**Evaluation style:** Gut + immediate value — will bounce instantly if the first look isn't useful; loves low-friction self-serve
**Common objections:**
- "I don't have time to learn another tool" → "Type in a competitor, see their newsletter footprint in one query. That's the whole onboarding."
- "Is this worth paying for?" → "Run the free sample lookup first. One good placement you'd otherwise have missed pays for it, and there's a 14-day money-back guarantee."

---

## How to Reach Them

**Best channel:** Email, Twitter/LinkedIn DMs, and the communities/newsletters they already read
**Best time:** Tuesday-Thursday business hours; DMs can work early morning or evening when founders are catching up asynchronously.
**What gets their attention:** "See where [a brand they admire / competitor] advertises" — concrete and aspirational
**What gets ignored:** Enterprise media-planning language, anything that smells like sales software

---

## Message Framework

**Value prop for this persona:**
Copy the newsletter-advertising playbook of brands like yours — see where they spend, then do the same, without guessing.

**Proof points that resonate before launch:**
- A recognizable peer brand's footprint shown instantly via the sample lookup
- "See a competitor's placements before you pay; money-back guarantee if it's not useful"

**Reference customers for this persona:** None yet — use peer-brand examples and live product evidence until design partners are referenceable.

---

## Sample Outreach Hook

> "Curious where brands like [peer brand] actually advertise? Here's every newsletter they sponsored this
> year — and the 3 that look like the best fit for you."
