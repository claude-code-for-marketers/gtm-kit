# Paid Ads Brief: Newsletter Sponsorship Intelligence
Date: 2026-06-17
Status: Draft ready for review

## Inputs

- Campaign goal: Get buy-side marketers to request early access for newsletter sponsorship intelligence.
- Persona: Head of Growth / Performance Marketing
- Landing page: `https://newsletterradar.com`
- CTA: Request early access / run a sample lookup
- Geography: United States
- Campaign status: Paused

## Audience

Growth and performance marketing leaders at B2B SaaS, AI, dev-tools, fintech, and business-productivity
companies that already run paid acquisition and are evaluating newsletters as a measurable channel.

## Angles

| Angle | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Competitor footprint | Buyers want to know where rivals are already advertising. |
| Retention proof | Sponsor tenure gives a public signal of whether placements are worth considering. |
| Budget protection | Newsletter buying is expensive enough that bad placements hurt. |
| Channel planning | Buyers need an exportable shortlist, not generic newsletter-ad advice. |

## Google Search Plan

- Campaign: `Newsletter Sponsorship Intelligence`
- Campaign type: Search
- Networks: Google Search
- Ad group: `Sponsorship Intelligence`
- Keyword themes: newsletter advertising intelligence, sponsorship data, competitor newsletter ads, newsletter media planning
- Negative intent to suppress: publisher sales, sponsor wanted listings, newsletter ad sales
- URL plan: UTM-tagged traffic to `https://newsletterradar.com`
- Upload files: `google/campaign.csv`, `google/ad_group.csv`, `google/responsive_search_ad.csv`, `google/keyword.csv`, and `google/ad_group_negative_keyword.csv`

## LinkedIn Plan

Create single-image ad variants around:

- competitor footprints
- retention as proof
- fewer trial-and-error placements

Images should use radar, map, sponsorship timeline, or media-planning motifs. Avoid fake dashboards,
customer logos, or precise performance metrics.

## Claim Boundaries

- Do not claim Newsletter Radar measures conversions, ROI, or attribution.
- Do not imply a free tier or free trial.
- Do not claim complete coverage of every newsletter.
- Do not frame the product as a lead-gen list or a publisher sales tool.
- Do not make competitor contrast the main pitch unless the ad angle explicitly names competitor research.
